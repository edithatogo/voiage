\section{Limitations}

The repository evidence establishes reproducible software behavior for tested
fixtures and supported environments; it does not establish fitness for every
decision context or replace domain validation. Users remain responsible for
model structure, input provenance, distributional assumptions,
willingness-to-pay thresholds, and interpretation.

Regression-based EVPPI and EVSI estimates inherit modeling choices and Monte
Carlo error. Users should examine convergence, effective sample size,
predictive adequacy, extrapolation, and sensitivity to method settings rather
than treating a scalar result as exact. Population VOI additionally depends on
eligible population, time horizon, discounting, implementation, and research
cost assumptions; \texttt{voiage} validates representations and numerical
preconditions but cannot determine whether those assumptions are substantively
appropriate.

The Python interface is broader than the current native R and Julia packages;
only EVPI has a direct v1 C-ABI route in both non-Python bindings. Optional
accelerator and experimental methods have narrower evidence boundaries than
the stable Rust-backed core. Accelerator, distributed, FPGA, and ASIC work is
evidence-gated and is not a production speedup claim. The repository's
automated checks reduce software risk but do not replace independent
statistical validation, code review, or replication with relevant domain data.

Authorship, affiliation, ORCID, funding, conflicts of interest, research-impact
evidence, arXiv category, license, and endorsement remain subject to final
human confirmation. A successful automated build does not imply arXiv upload,
moderation, announcement, journal submission, review, or acceptance.
