\section{Applications and intended use}

In health technology assessment, the stable methods quantify parameter and
sample information for specified designs. The wider framework includes
fixture-backed formulations for adaptive study designs and separate
formulations for the value of research and the value of implementation.
Heterogeneity and distributional methods compare a population-average strategy
with subgroup-specific or equity-weighted choices. Perspective analysis can
assess whether a health-system, payer, patient, or societal framing changes
the preferred strategy rather than treating perspective as an invisible model
setting.

For environmental and energy decisions, the same framework is intended to compare
monitoring, mitigation, conservation, or investment options under uncertain
effects, costs, thresholds, and delayed evidence. Engineering and manufacturing
applications can represent inspection, reliability, design refinement, or quality
information where failures and irreversible commitments are costly. Finance
and portfolio applications can represent staged evidence, waiting value,
lock-in, and competing research opportunities. These uses require
domain-appropriate outcome models and value functions; the package supplies
decision analysis, not domain facts.

The frontier also includes formulations for evidence-governance questions:
whether validation data could
change deployment, whether source evidence transports to a target population,
whether linkage or measurement improvements justify their cost and privacy
burden, and whether evidence has become obsolete enough to warrant
refresh. These formulations can serve as explicit decision records, while
their fixture-backed maturity indicates the need for further validation.

Version 1.0.0 and its examples are available from
\url{https://github.com/edithatogo/voiage/releases/tag/v1.0.0}. Intended uses do
not constitute evidence of adoption or policy impact, and no example replaces
validation with relevant domain data and decision-makers.
