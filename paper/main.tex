\begin{abstract}
Value of Information (VOI) analysis helps decision-makers judge whether
reducing uncertainty is worth the cost. Many analyses focus on uncertain
numerical inputs but do not show whether the answer could also change because
of the model used, differences between population groups, the viewpoint
adopted, poor implementation, weak evidence, or the timing of a decision.
\texttt{voiage} provides a common way to describe and analyse these connected
questions. Version 1.0.0 calculates established perfect, partial, and sample
information measures and provides developing analyses for model, population,
implementation, evidence, and timing uncertainty. In a synthetic health
example, the programme is preferred in 49.24\% of simulations at the reference
threshold; uncertainty about health effect has greater partial value than
uncertainty about cost, and delayed incomplete uptake lowers the net value of
a proposed study. The software retains choices, populations, assumptions,
warnings, and data sources with the result. This paper explains the economic
questions represented by the package, the interpretation of its information
measures, and the validation still needed for newer methods.
\end{abstract}
