# Comprehensive Literature Review of VOI Methods

## Core VOI Methods

### 1. Expected Value of Perfect Information (EVPI)
**Original Reference**: Raiffa, H. (1968). Decision Analysis: Introductory Lectures on Choices Under Uncertainty. Addison-Wesley.

**Key Developments**:
- Claxton, K. (1999). The irrelevance of inference: a decision-making approach to the stochastic evaluation of health care technologies. Journal of Health Economics, 18(3), 341-364.

### 2. Expected Value of Partial Perfect Information (EVPPI)
**Original Reference**: Yokota, F. T., & Thompson, K. M. (2004). Value of information analysis in environmental health risk management decisions. Risk Analysis, 24(3), 619-631.

**Key Developments**:
- Strong, M., & Oakley, J. E. (2014). Estimating the expected value of partial perfect information in health economic evaluations using integrated nested Laplace approximation. Medical Decision Making, 34(3), 390-400.

### 3. Expected Value of Sample Information (EVSI)
**Original Reference**: Thompson, K. M., & Graham, J. D. (1996). Going beyond expected value: a methodological framework for valuing imperfect information in health risk management. Risk Analysis, 16(1), 57-68.

**Key Developments**:
- Ades, A. E., Lu, G., & Claxton, K. (2003). Expected value of sample information calculations in medical decision modeling. Medical Decision Making, 24(2), 207-220.

## Advanced VOI Methods

### 4. Structural Uncertainty VOI
**Key References**:
- Jackson, C. H., Ades, A. E., & Welton, N. J. (2010). How does PoF meta-regression deal with structural and parameter uncertainty? A worked comparison to a reference Bayesian approach. Research Synthesis Methods, 1(1-2), 93-105.

### 5. Network Meta-Analysis VOI
**Key References**:
- Dias, S., Welton, N. J., Caldwell, D. M., & Ades, A. E. (2010). Checking consistency in mixed treatment comparison meta-analysis. Statistics in Medicine, 29(7-8), 932-944.

### 6. Adaptive Design VOI
**Key References**:
- Jennison, C., & Turnbull, B. W. (2000). Group sequential methods with applications to clinical trials. Chapman & Hall/CRC.

### 7. Portfolio Optimization
**Key References**:
- Eckermann, S., Briggs, A., & Willan, A. R. (2008). Health technology assessment in the cost-utility plane. Medical Decision Making, 28(6), 792-802.

### 8. Value of Heterogeneity
**Key References**:
- Espinoza, M. A., Manca, A., Claxton, K., & Sculpher, M. J. (2014). The value of heterogeneity for cost-effectiveness subgroup analysis: conceptual framework and application. Medical Decision Making, 34(8), 951-964.

## Emerging VOI Methods

### 9. Real Options Valuation in Healthcare
**Key References**:
- Mariano, B. S., Ferreira, J. J., & Godinho, M. (2013). Real options in healthcare investments: literature review and research orientations. The Scientific World Journal, 2013.

### 10. Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (MCDA) VOI
**Key References**:
- Thokala, P., & Dyer, J. (2016). The analytical hierarchy process: should it be used in healthcare evaluation? PharmacoEconomics, 34(7), 655-667.

### 11. Dynamic VOI
**Key References**:
- Alarid-Escudero, F., Enns, E. A., Kuntz, K. M., Michaud, T. L., & Jalal, H. (2018). Time travel in value of information analysis: the value of perfect information in modeling and decision making. Risk Analysis, 38(11), 2384-2398.

### 12. Observational Data VOI
**Key References**:
- Baio, G., & Dawid, A. P. (2015). Probabilistic sensitivity analysis in health economics. Statistical Methods in Medical Research, 24(6), 615-634.

### 13. Calibration VOI
**Key References**:
- Kennedy, M. C., & O'Hagan, A. (2001). Bayesian calibration of computer models. Journal of the Royal Statistical Society: Series B (Statistical Methodology), 63(3), 425-464.

### 14. Sequential VOI
**Key References**:
- Eckermann, S., Willan, A. R., & Briggs, A. H. (2004). Sequential research designs for economic evaluation: the value of information for research design. Health Economics, 13(2), 193-201.

### 15. Value of Correlation Information
**Key References**:
- Coyle, D., & Oakley, J. E. (2008). Estimating the expected value of partial perfect information: a review of methods and software. Medical Decision Making, 28(3), 421-433.

### 16. Value of Distributional Information
**Key References**:
- Oakley, J. E., & O'Hagan, A. (2006). Probabilistic partial evaluation: the expected value of distributional information. Journal of Multivariate Analysis, 97(3), 592-607.

### 17. Value of Model Improvement
**Key References**:
- Jalal, H., & Alarid-Escudero, F. (2018). A generalization of the format approach for assessing the value of reductions in model input uncertainty. Operations Research Letters, 46(1), 120-125.

### 18. Multi-Parameter EVPPI Methods
**Key References**:
- Heath, A., Man, K. K. C., & Baio, G. (2018). voi: The Expected Value of Partial Perfect Information. R package version 1.0.2.
